Ian Page - Computer Systems Produced

 

Handel: A hardware compilation system for producing highly parallel hardware implementations directly from software programs - now exploited commercially through Celoxica.

 

HARP: A reconfigurable computer based on FPGAs, intimately linked to a RISC microprocessor; followed by a number of other FPGA-based computing platforms - exploited commercially.

 

ValCard and ValTram Systems: developed in collaboration with the Control Engineering Group in Engineering Science at Oxford for the rapid implementation of new sensor/effector/control systems - exploited commercially.

 

Disputer: A dual paradigm (SIMD/MIMD) processor with over 300 processors for fast graphics and vision processing.

 

DisArray: A 256-processor parallel computer for computer graphics.

 

RasterBlaster: An ASIC designed for the fundamental 'RasterOp' computer graphics operation; mask set produced at Rutherford Laboratory; chip fabricated at Edinburgh University.

  

VLSI CAD system: A novel and highly interactive CAD tool for designing chips and containing a geometrical silicon compiler. Built to design the chip above, it ran on the world's first commercial, workstation. Used (for a time) by over 20 UK universities until its platform, the Three Rivers 'Perq' system, was discontinued.

 

PocSy: (Piece of Cake System!) A fast bitmap display to turn a PDP11 into a workstation; our attempt to build a Xerox Alto with no money!

 

Computer-Based Training System: An extensive prototype software system for an advanced CBT system commissioned by ICL. Later spun off as a separate company.

 

Multi-media workstation: A 32-bit microprogrammed computer system with fast, high-quality colour graphics for the CBT project.

 

Simulset: An extensive design specification for an advanced computer-based animation system for television and video studios to generate complex simulated scenes, which could be integrated with live-action material, thus removing some need for building studio sets.
