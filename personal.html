Ian Page - Personal Details

This page is mainly personal information about me. Its all vanity of course - I’ll be amazed if anyone actually reads this!

When not working, I enjoy singing - choral and solo; restoring juke boxes, pinball machines and other mechanical toys, making wooden bowls, children’s toys, and   having fun with wood turning. I enjoy cooking, especially Indian food; collecting and eating wild mushrooms. Lesser interests have included amateur dramatics and fire eating.

I enjoy singing; anything from choral music with 3,000 others in the Albert Hall or singing solo traditional English songs at various clubs and sessions around Oxfordshire; often unaccompanied, but also I play guitar and English concertina alongside.

Brief History

I am originally from Rochdale in Lancashire - home of the Co-operative Movement and the Rochdale Canal. Having grown up within sight of the canal, I am very pleased recently to have acquired my own 57 foot narrow boat, Dulce Domum, which is based on the Regent’s Canal in London

I studied Electronic and Electrical Engineering at King's College, London and Systems Engineering at City University, London. I spent the first few years of my working life in industrial/commercial settings with International Computers Ltd, the University of Bradford Computer Centre, and Plessey Ltd. in programmer and team leader roles.

I then moved into academic life and joined the Department of Computer Science at Queen Mary College, London University as a lecturer and researcher. After eight years at QMC, I moved to the Computing Laboratory at Oxford University where I was a Reader in Computing Science.

I am now the Business development Director of Seven Spires Investments, a new £25m early-stage, high-tech investment fund. I am also an independent consultant and entrepreneur, part-time consultant to Celoxica, Visiting Professor in the Department of Computing at Imperial College and Entrepreneur in Residence at the University of Reading.
