Projects for Undergraduate and M.Sc. Students

In general, the projects here are in fact descriptions of entire families of projects. They can vary in complexity from those suitable for undergraduate projects to those suitable for M.Sc. or Ph.D. students. Factors that they all hopefully have in common are that there is a real research component to them, that they are challenging, that they are related to issues of real commercial interest, and that they may be a great deal of fun.

The Hardware Compilation Projects are similar in style and scope to many I have run at Oxford University over the last ten years as part of the research effort that has resulted in a commercial hardware compilation system (Handel-C) and a successful international company (Celoxica Ltd.) now developing and exploiting it. You should also look at the projects offered by Wayne Luk who was also part of the Oxford research group and has his own range of excellent Hardware Compilation projects.

The Max Headroom Projects represent a new theme that I would like to develop. Like the Hardware Compilation Projects were, these are also centred round a ‘Grand Challenge’ which is long term vision of something that we will not be able to develop to its full extent for decades, but will yet provide guidance for a whole series of exciting projects.
